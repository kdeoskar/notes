\subsection*{Example: Van der Waal's gas}

\vskip 0.5cm
When does Van der Waal's equation
\[ \left( P + a \left( \frac{N}{V} \right)^2 \right)\left( V - nb \right) = N k_B T \]
approximately describe an ideal gas in terms of $\frac{V}{N}$?

\vskip 0.5cm
\begin{dottedbox}
  The Ideal Gas Law reads as 
\[ P V = N k_B T \]

So, in order for the Van der Waal's equation to be approximately the same, 

\begin{itemize}
  \item We expect $V / N >> $ something
  \item In particular, we want $V - Nb \approx V$, so 
  \[ V >> Nb \implies \boxed{\frac{V}{N} >> b } \]
  \item We also require 
  \[ P >> a\left( \frac{N}{V} \right)^2 \implies \frac{V}{N} >> \sqrt{\frac{a}{p}} \]
\end{itemize}
\end{dottedbox}
