\begin{bluebox}
  \textbf{Question 1:} Let $X$ be the mapping torus of a degree$-2$ mp of $(\sph^2, \mathrm{pt})$ to itself (i.e. obtained from the cylinder $\sph^2 \times [0, 1]$ by gluing its boundaries by this degree$-2$ map). Show that the embedding $i$ of $\sph^1$ into $X$ as the interval $\mathrm{pt} \times [0, 1]$ glued at the endpoints induces an isomorphism in homology but is not a homotopy equivalence. 
\end{bluebox}

\vskip 0.5cm
\textbf{\underline{Solution:}}
\\
\\
Consider the mapping torus $X$ is the space $$ X =  \bigslant{\sph^2 \times [0, 1]}{(t, 0) \sim (f(t), 1)} $$ where $f \text{ : } \sph^2 \rightarrow \sph^2$ is  \textbf{degree 2 map} and we consider the embedding $i \text{ : } \sph^1 \rightarrow X$ given by identifying $$\sph^1 \approx \bigslant{x \times [0, 1]}{(x, 0) \sim (x, 1)}$$ for some $x \in X$.
